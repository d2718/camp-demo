Course Symbols

Each Mathematics course in the system has a unique short chunk of letters and numbers that is used to uniquely identify it. This helps differentiate, say, Honors Algebra I using the Discovering Algebra book from the Mathspace Honors Algebra I course. These appear in the "Goal Details" dialog when editing or adding a pace goal for a student:

Goal Details

And they are also required when adding goals in bulk by uploading a CSV file. But

You needn't remember them!

If you start typing the name of a course (e.g., algebra) in the "course" input of the "Goal Details" dialog, a little dropdown box should auto-suggest some courses (including the one you want), and you can just select the appropriate course that way.

Also, the lower-right-hand corner of the Teacher's view should contain a little upward-pointing chevron in a box:

Clicking on that box should toggle the expansion of a list of all courses in the system and their symbols.

Score Representations

Chapter goal and Final Exam scores can be expressed in three different ways:

As a decimal:
→ (93%)

As a percent:
→ (85%)

As a fraction:
→ (90%)

The software will try its best to guess which of the three you mean.

In case you really care:
  • If the score has a fraction in it, it will be calculated by dividing the top by the bottom.
  • If the value is less than 2.0, it will be interpreted as a decimal (that is, a score out of 1).
  • If the value is greater than 2.0, it will be interpreted as a percent (that is, a score out of 100).

CSV of Goals

Adding goals one at a time with the button is intuitive but tedious. Goals can be added in bulk by uploading a CSV file. The format of the CSV file is as follows:

  • One goal per row.
  • Rows that start with an octothorpe (#) are ignored, so you write reminders to yourself, (e.g., to help remember the column order).
  • Speaking of column order, it is:
    uname, course symbol, chapter #, year, month, day, review, incomplete
  • Any text in either the review or incomplete columns will mark the given goal as such; leaving the given column blank leaves that property unmarked.
  • Any other column can be left blank (except chapter#), and its value will be inferred from previous line.

The spreadsheet excerpt above shows a series of goals for the student with user name jsmith.

  • The first row is ignored because it starts with #, and serves only to remind us of the order of the columns as we fill them out.
  • The first goal is a review of Chapter 9 of Algebra I (from the Prentice Hall Algebra 1 book).
  • The second goal is Chapter 10 of Geometry (from the Prentice Hall Geometry book.)
  • The next 10 goals are the 10 chapters of Core Algebra II from the Prentice Hall Algebra 2 textbook.

The Notices and Exam Score inputs should be self-explanatory. The "Exam Fraction" inputs indicate what portion a student's total semester grade is made up by the Final Exam grade. For Upper-School students this should more or less always be 0.2 (because US exams are unilaterally 20% of a student's grade), but this may differ for some Middle-Grades students.

The button will save the values of the six inputs in the footer.

The button will (shocker) delete all of the given student's goals.

Finally, will distribute the due dates of the given student's goals evenly throughout the academic year.

The "autopace" function only works on goals that already have due dates. Goals without due dates will be ignored.
